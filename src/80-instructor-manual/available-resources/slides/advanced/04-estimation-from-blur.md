# Estimation from Motion Blur

Cameras are integrative sensors, as they sum up incoming light during the duration of the exposure. One central challenge in [state estimation](slides-and-recordings-estimation-overview) when using a [camera](slides-and-recordings-vision-overview) is that the relative motion of the camera and the scene will induce motion blur in the resulting image. While motion blur is typically considered undesirable because it makes features of interest more difficult to detect, it is possible to use this blur as an asset rather than a nuisance.
